Retrospective Artifact

A retro on my own practice

Any SM who can't run a retro on themselves isn't really running retros. Here's mine. Honest.

✓
What went well
Ceremony quality Standups end on time. Sprint planning ends with a committed, realistic goal. Every ceremony produces an artifact or a decision — not just attendance.
PO partnership Backlogs stay healthy. I treat refinement as a practice, not a meeting. POs stop dreading it when it's run with discipline.
Jira as a tool, not a chore Teams I've left still use the dashboards I built. That's the metric.
Difficult conversations I'll tell a team their velocity is fiction. I'll tell a stakeholder scope creep is killing the sprint. Kindly. Clearly. With data.
△
What I'd improve
Patience with slow adopters I move fast toward good process. Teams operating without discipline for years need more runway than I sometimes give them.
Documentation timing I write the thing, then document it. Working on making documentation part of the definition of done — not an afterthought.
Delegation vs. doing When something's broken, I fix it. Building the habit of coaching teams to fix it themselves takes deliberate restraint. Still working on it.
→

Working Agreements Draft v0.1

What I bring to the first team session

A starting point — to be rewritten by the team, together. The SM's job is to show up with a draft, not a policy.

01
Ceremonies start and end on time
Respecting time is respecting each other. We don't wait for late arrivals and we don't run over for unprepared ones.
02
Definition of Ready is a gate, not a suggestion
A story without clear acceptance criteria doesn't enter sprint planning. Full stop. The PO knows this. The team expects it.
03
Blockers surface in standup, not after
If you knew yesterday, say it today. The SM removes blockers — but only the ones we know about.
04
Retro actions have owners and due dates
We don't generate a list and move on. Each action gets a name and a sprint to close. Reviewed at the top of the next retro.
05
Jira is the source of truth
Not Slack, not memory. If it's not in the ticket, it didn't happen. Status updated same day. We don't narrate work that isn't tracked.
06
The SM protects the sprint goal, not individual tickets
New work during a sprint goes through the PO and gets evaluated against what we committed to — not absorbed quietly by a willing engineer.
